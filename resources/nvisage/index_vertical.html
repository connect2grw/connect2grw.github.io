ABOUT US

At N'visage, we believe that economic sustainability and social empowerment are the keys for community development. We partner with marginalized artisan communities in India, providing them with sustainable employment and fair wages to help improve their economic and social standings. These artisan communities are rich with unique, traditional art forms but lack access to and knowledge of outside markets and modern design trends. We aim to bridge this gap by providing the artisans with the necessary resources to produce functional goods that incorporate their traditional arts with the design trends.

The Dhokra metal-casting uses the lost-wax casting technique that was probably used by the Mohenjodaro craftsman to cast the bronze figurine of a dancing girl some 4500 years ago. This unbroken tradition coupled with the intrinsic starkness and vitality of the art that makes Dhokra a coveted collector’s item in India and abroad for connoisseurs and laypersons alike. The Dhokra people are settled over a vast tract in the mineral rich central Indian belt of Orissa, Jharkhand, and Chhatisgarh.

Gond paintings done on village mud walls use colors derived from charcoal, colored soil, plant sap, leaves, and cow dung and can last up to 20 years. The speciality of Gond panting is the simplicity of the ideas and their representation of the day-to-day life of rural people. Today, paintings for sale are usually done on canvas and paper. The Gond are the largest tribe, and original inhabitants, of India, but the Baiga tribe are also known for their paintings.

This is amongst the oldest and most widespread craft. Historical records of prehistoric era have been found in the remains of pottery. It is believed to have existed since 7000 BC in the Neolithic period. Among the clay products of Uttar Pradesh, pottery of Gorakpur is very famous. The potters of this region make animal figures like horses and elephants with hand-appliquéd ornamentation, figures of gods and goddesses, lamps, and other ritual objects are all crafted by hand.
